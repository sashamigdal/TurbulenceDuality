Euler Ensemble Proof

Theorem

The Euler ensemble solves the discrete Momentum Loop Equation (MLE).

Proof

We start from the general Ansatz with real vectors \(\vec A, \vec f_k\), corresponding to the real circulation:

\[\vec F_k = \I \vec A + (\vec f_{k -1} + \vec f_k)/2\] \[\Delta \vec F_k = \vec f_{k} - \vec f_{k-1}\] \[(\vec f_{k} - \vec f_{k-1})^2=1\]

Analyzing the imaginary and real parts of the second equation in the system, we observe that the imaginary part will vanish provided:

\[\vec A \cdot \vec f_k =0 \;\forall k\] \[\vec f_k^2 = \vec f_{k-1}^2 \;\forall k\]

We conclude that \(\vec f_k\) belongs to a circle with some radius \(R\) in the origin of the plane, which plane is orthogonal to \(\vec A\).

In the coordinate frame where \(\vec A = \{0,0,A\}\):

\[\vec f_k = R \left\{\cos(\alpha_k), \sin(\alpha_k), 0\right\}\]

The \(SO(3)\) matrix needed to rotate our vectors to this coordinate frame can be absorbed into the rotation matrix \(\Omega\) we have in our solution.

The radius \(R\) and \(A\) are determined by the real part of our equations as follows:

\[4 A^2 = 2 R^2 \left(1 + \cos(\alpha_{k} - \alpha_{k-1})\right)\] \[1 = 2 R^2 \left(1 - \cos(\alpha_{k} - \alpha_{k-1})\right)\]

Solving these equations, we find the \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) variables at every step:

\[\alpha_{k} = \alpha_{k-1} + \beta \sigma_k,\; \sigma_k^2 =1\]

The radius \(R\) and the length \(A = |\vec A|\) are related to this angular step \(\beta\):

\[R = \frac{1}{2 \sin \left(\frac{\beta}{2}\right)}\] \[A = \frac{1}{2 \tan \left(\frac{\beta}{2}\right)}\]

The periodicity of the sequence \(\vec f_k\) requires the angular step to be a fraction of \(2\pi\), which brings us to the Euler ensemble.
