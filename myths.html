Introduction

More than eighty years ago, Kolmogorov and Obukhov made a breakthrough in turbulence theory by establishing the relation for the three-point correlation function of the velocity field in turbulent flow. However, this formula only selects the potential part of the triple velocity correlation function, missing critical aspects of rotational turbulence.

Challenging K41 Scaling Laws

The classical Kolmogorov scaling laws (K41) assume a universal energy cascade and constant energy flux. However, our findings suggest that the energy dissipation occurs directly in micro-structures (Burgers filaments), rather than cascading down the inertial range.

\[ \langle \varphi_\alpha(\vec{r}_0)\,\varphi_\beta(\vec{r}_0)\,\omega_\nu(\vec{r}+\vec{r}_0) \rangle = e_{\mu\gamma\nu}\,\partial_\mu(\vec{r})\,\langle \varphi_\alpha(\vec{r}_0)\,\varphi_\beta(\vec{r}_0)\,v_\nu(\vec{r}+\vec{r}_0) \rangle \]

This suggests that **K41 does not correctly describe rotational turbulence**, contradicting its assumed universality.

Reevaluation of Experimental Data

Decades of experimental and DNS results [5] show deviations from K41 scaling, yet many researchers continue to fit data into Kolmogorov models rather than questioning the assumptions. For example, recent studies show a strong deviation from the \( k^{-5/3} \) scaling at large scales, with significant curvature in log-log plots indicating non-trivial universal functions rather than simple power laws.

Beyond Scaling Laws: A New Theoretical Framework

Our approach replaces heuristic scaling laws with **exact analytical solutions derived from first principles**. The Euler Ensemble formulation provides a solvable turbulence model that naturally predicts the observed deviations from K41. This suggests that turbulence theory requires a fundamental revision.
